\title{The CGRP Ligand--Receptor Asymmetry in Migraine Genetics is Reproducible Across Five Analytical Lenses}
\author{Julian Quick}
\date{\today}

\begin{document}
\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
The CGRP ligand locus (\textit{CALCA}/\textit{CALCB}) reaches genome-wide significance in the largest published migraine GWAS, while the CGRP receptor-complex genes (\textit{CALCRL}, \textit{RAMP1}, \textit{CRCP}) do not. Hautakangas et al.\ (2022) reported this single-paragraph observation in passing. We consolidate it across five analytical lenses applied to the same and adjacent migraine genetic data---variant-level GWAS, statistical fine-mapping, eQTL-integrated TWAS, PPI network proximity to the CGRP-selective receptor anchor, and OpenTargets Genetics aggregated locus-to-gene evidence---and report two findings. First, the asymmetry persists at every lens, with consistent direction across methods that share much of the underlying GWAS data but apply different inferential machinery. Second, the asymmetry is graded rather than binary: \textit{CALCA} carries canonical-GWAS-hit-tier OpenTargets genetic-association evidence ($0.753$, in the range of \textit{TRPM8}, \textit{FHL5}, \textit{PRDM16}), \textit{CALCB} is moderate ($0.450$), and \textit{CALCRL}, \textit{RAMP1}, and \textit{CRCP} have no \texttt{genetic\_association} datatype recorded at all in the OpenTargets evidence ledger despite being well-represented via drug-target and literature channels. The asymmetry is no longer a single-paper observation. It is a constraint that any causal model of CGRP-pathway involvement in migraine genetics must accommodate. Code, pre-registration, raw-data hashes, and additional analyses (autonomic over-representation, GTEx tissue expression, ESR1 ChIP-seq) are at \url{https://github.com/kilojoules/migraine-cgrp-pots-synthesis}.
\end{abstract}

\section{The observation}

The CGRP system carries the strongest perturbational evidence for causal involvement in migraine of any molecular target: ligand- and receptor-targeting biologics and small molecules are FDA-approved migraine therapies and reduce monthly migraine days by 3.5--4.7 in episodic and 4.3--4.6 in chronic migraine across pivotal phase-3 trials~\cite{goadsby_2017_strive_erenumab,croop_2019_rimegepant,ailani_2021_advance_atogepant,edvinsson_2018_cgrp_review}. Genetic evidence is asymmetric across the same pathway. Hautakangas et al.\ (2022) reported the largest published migraine GWAS ($102{,}084$ cases, 123 risk loci) and noted in passing that ``\textit{CALCRL}, \textit{RAMP1}, and \textit{RCP} show no statistically comparable association (all $P > 10^{-4}$)'' to the genome-wide-significant CGRP ligand locus on chromosome 11~\cite{hautakangas_2022_migraine_gwas}. The observation has not been treated as a hypothesis worth testing in its own right.

We do that here. The question is narrow: does the asymmetry persist when the same and adjacent migraine genetic data are interrogated through inferential machinery distinct from the variant-level GWAS that surfaced it? If the asymmetry is an artifact of the test (e.g., locus-level $P$-value resolution, or proximity-based gene assignment), at least one of four other lenses should disagree. If it is a genuine feature of the genetic architecture, all five should converge.

\section{Five lenses}

We summarise the per-gene evidence across five lenses in Table~\ref{tab:asymmetry}. \paragraph{(i) Variant-level GWAS.} Hautakangas et al.\ (2022) report locus-level $P$-values for 123 risk loci~\cite{hautakangas_2022_migraine_gwas}. The CGRP ligand locus on chromosome 11 (locus 75) reaches genome-wide significance; the receptor-complex genes \textit{CALCRL}, \textit{RAMP1}, and \textit{CRCP} do not, with all reported sub-genome-wide-significance $P$-values exceeding $10^{-4}$.

\paragraph{(ii) Statistical fine-mapping.} The 2024 fine-mapping follow-up of $98{,}374$ migraine cases identified 181 sets of candidate causal variants and prioritized causal genes via FOCUS~\cite{hautakangas_2025_migraine_finemapping}. \textit{CALCA} and \textit{CALCB} are FOCUS-prioritized at locus 75; \textit{CALCRL}, \textit{RAMP1}, and \textit{CRCP} are not nominated.

\paragraph{(iii) eQTL-integrated TWAS.} Ghaffar and Nyholt (2023) ran a TWAS integrating GTEx eQTLs across 49 tissues with the migraine GWAS summary statistics~\cite{ghaffar_2023_migraine_eqtl_twas}. The CGRP ligand genes are surfaced; the receptor-complex genes are not.

\paragraph{(iv) PPI network proximity.} We compute the closest-distance proximity statistic of Guney et al.~\cite{guney_2016_proximity} from the 123 lead-SNP-nearest migraine GWAS genes to the CGRP-selective receptor anchor $T = \{\text{\textit{CALCRL}}, \text{\textit{RAMP1}}\}$, on the union of BioGRID 5.0.256 and IntAct 2026-01-10 physical-only interactions ($22{,}091$ nodes, $1{,}109{,}901$ edges, largest connected component). With a degree-binned random gene-set null and $1{,}000$ permutations (seed $20260426$), the migraine signal sits significantly \emph{further} from the CGRP receptor than chance ($d_c = 2.50$, $z = +3.54$, two-sided $p = 0.004$). Three of four pre-registered specificity controls (psoriasis, essential hypertension, type 2 diabetes) show the same direction at similar magnitude, so the test does not establish migraine specificity; it does, however, confirm that the migraine common-variant signal does not reach the receptor module at short distance, in a graph that does not encode literature bias (STRING text-mining channel excluded). \textit{CALCRL} has only 6 PPI partners in the merged graph and sits in a sparse region of the interactome---a structural condition that bounds what proximity tests can detect at the receptor side regardless of the disease genetics.

\paragraph{(v) OpenTargets locus-to-gene.} The OpenTargets Genetics platform~\cite{ochoa_2023_opentargets} aggregates fine-mapped credible-set, eQTL/sQTL colocalization, chromatin-interaction, and distance-based evidence from multiple GWAS sources into per-target genetic-association scores. Querying the platform's GraphQL API for migraine (MONDO\_0005277) returns $2{,}541$ associated targets. \textit{CALCA}'s genetic-association score ($0.753$) is in the range of canonical migraine GWAS positive controls (\textit{PRDM16} $0.798$, \textit{TRPM8} $0.816$, \textit{FHL5} $0.855$, \textit{MEF2D} $0.597$). \textit{CALCB} is moderate ($0.450$). \textit{CALCRL}, \textit{RAMP1}, and \textit{CRCP} have no \texttt{genetic\_association} datatype recorded in the OpenTargets evidence ledger at all; the non-zero overall scores for \textit{CALCRL} ($0.607$) and \textit{RAMP1} ($0.571$) derive entirely from drug-target evidence (anti-CGRP-receptor antibodies and gepants) and literature mining. Verified by inspection of the raw API response: \texttt{datatype\_scores} for these three genes contains \texttt{literature} and \texttt{clinical} keys but does not contain \texttt{genetic\_association}.

\section{The asymmetry is graded, not binary}

\textit{CALCA} sits at the canonical-GWAS-hit tier across all five lenses. \textit{CALCB} is consistently weaker on the genetics side (OpenTargets L2G $0.450$ vs $0.753$ for \textit{CALCA}) but still positive. \textit{CALCRL}, \textit{RAMP1}, and \textit{CRCP} are at zero genetic-association evidence across all five lenses. The asymmetry is therefore graded along the ligand--receptor axis (with \textit{CALCB} intermediate), not a strict ligand-vs-receptor dichotomy. This graded structure is itself informative: it suggests that whatever drives the asymmetry acts unevenly even within the ligand pair, and is not a single switch between ``signal'' and ``no signal.''

\section{What the asymmetry means, and what it does not}

We do not adjudicate \emph{why} the receptor side is invisible to common-variant signal in migraine. Three explanations remain compatible with the data: (a)~purifying selection on essential receptor function---CGRP receptor signaling has broad cardiovascular roles~\cite{russell_2014_cgrp_physiology}, and post-marketing erenumab is associated with blood-pressure elevation~\cite{saely_2021_erenumab_hypertension}, so common large-effect variants on \textit{CALCRL}/\textit{RAMP1} may be selected against; (b)~allelic-series concentration on the ligand locus, with the receptor side contributing primarily through rare or structural variation that current common-variant GWAS does not power; (c)~sparse interactome and limited gene-mapping resolution at the receptor side, which applies specifically to lens (iv) (\textit{CALCRL} has 6 PPI partners, below the $\sim 25$--$50\%$ module-coverage threshold of Menche et al.~\cite{menche_2015_diseaseome}) and only marginally to the others. These are not separable at the resolution available.

What the asymmetry does \emph{not} establish is that the CGRP receptor complex is mechanistically uninvolved in migraine. Anti-CGRP-receptor antibodies and small-molecule receptor antagonists work~\cite{goadsby_2017_strive_erenumab,croop_2019_rimegepant,ailani_2021_advance_atogepant}, so receptor-level perturbation modulates the disease. The asymmetry is therefore a feature of \emph{common-variant genetic architecture}, not of biology, and any causal model of CGRP-pathway involvement in migraine genetics must accommodate it.

\section{Reproducibility}

Pre-registration, scripts, raw-data SHA-256 hashes, and additional analyses (focused autonomic over-representation, g:Profiler enrichment, GTEx tissue-distribution heatmap, ESR1 ChIP-seq promoter occupancy on CGRP-pathway genes, full OpenTargets per-gene scores) are at \url{https://github.com/kilojoules/migraine-cgrp-pots-synthesis}. Random seeds are fixed at $20260426$ (PPI proximity) and $20260427$ (post-hoc analyses). The longer manuscript with the full POTS-comorbidity framing is preserved in the repository's git history; this note isolates the load-bearing observation.
